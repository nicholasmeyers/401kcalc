# Future Roadmap

Concise planning notes for where `401kcalc` should invest next.

## Core strategy

- Use broad calculators and guides to earn search traffic and trust.
- Use decision tools and workflows to capture higher-intent users.
- Prioritize features that help users answer "what should I do next?"
- Keep the site clean and trustworthy; avoid monetization that weakens product quality.

## Product buckets

### Traffic drivers

- 401(k) calculator
- Retirement-by-age benchmarks
- Guides on contribution limits, Roth vs traditional, employer match, and age milestones
- Search-friendly education pages tied to real retirement questions

### Revenue-intent workflows

- Job-change / old 401(k) rollover decisions
- Roth vs traditional contribution decision support
- Backdoor Roth and pro-rata rule workflow
- Solo 401(k) vs SEP IRA workflow
- Contribution priority across 401(k), IRA, and HSA
- Retirement income gap / catch-up planning

## Best ROI feature ranking

| Priority | Feature / workflow | Traffic value | Revenue value | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Old 401(k) rollover decision tool | Medium | High | Strong action intent and natural IRA / rollover partner fit |
| 2 | Roth vs traditional 401(k) decision tool | High | High | Big search demand and a real decision users struggle with |
| 3 | Solo 401(k) vs SEP IRA workflow | Medium | High | Self-employed setup is high intent and commercially relevant |
| 4 | 401(k) / IRA / HSA contribution order tool | Medium | High | Useful for higher-income users deciding where the next dollar goes |
| 5 | Backdoor Roth + pro-rata calculator | Medium | High | Strong tax intent and good fit for IRA / tax tool partners |
| 6 | Retirement income gap / catch-up planner | Medium | Medium-High | Strong user value, especially for ages 50+ |
| 7 | Can-I-retire-at-X planner | High | Medium | Likely traffic winner, but slightly softer monetization intent |
| 8 | Household / spouse retirement planner | Medium | Medium | Valuable long-term differentiator and good premium feature candidate |

## Near-term focus

Build next:

1. Old 401(k) rollover workflow
2. Roth vs traditional decision tool
3. Solo 401(k) vs SEP IRA workflow

Why:

- These are close to real account-opening or rollover decisions.
- They fit future partner / referral monetization without harming trust.
- They complement the current calculator instead of duplicating it.

## Medium-term focus

Build after the top three:

1. Contribution order planner
2. Backdoor Roth workflow
3. Retirement income gap / catch-up planner

Why:

- These deepen topical authority.
- They create more guides and internal-linking opportunities.
- They broaden monetizable intent without making the site feel sales-y.

## Long-term bets

- Household / spouse planning
- Saved scenarios and comparisons
- Downloadable or shareable planning summaries
- More advanced withdrawal and retirement income planning

Why:

- Better product moat
- Better repeat usage
- Better premium-feature potential

## Rules for future features

- Prefer workflows over simple number-in / number-out calculators.
- Tie every new tool to at least one guide cluster.
- Favor features that support a real financial decision or next step.
- Do not add tools that are broad but weak on intent or differentiation.
- Maintain a clean UX even when monetization arrives later.
